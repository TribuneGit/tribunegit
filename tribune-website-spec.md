## 0. Positioning Brief (read first, governs every page)

**What the public site says Tribune is:**
Tribune builds AI agents for businesses. We design them, train them on your real work, deploy them inside the tools your team already uses, and then hand you the keys. You own the system. It runs without us. That is the point.

**The moat vs. marketing rule (permanent, non-negotiable):**
- Internal truth (the moat): an AI builds other AI agents, and gets better with every build.
- Public message (the marketing): *we build AI agents for you.* A service, delivered by a firm.
- The website NEVER states, implies, or winks at the internal truth. No "the asset is the builder" framing, no AI C-suite roster, no meta-agent language. "We" on the website means the firm and its founders. Full stop.

**Primary domain:** TribuneOS.com (TribuneInc.com redirects).

**Tagline system (from the brand copy library, altitude rules apply):**
- SIGNATURE (the manifesto, verbatim): **We win when you no longer need us.** Used in the About close and the Doctrine close. Never on a page that carries a CAMPAIGN line.
- LOCKUP (footers, tight spaces): **Power that stays. Help that leaves.**
- DESCRIPTOR (subheads, meta descriptions): **We build your AI organization, then hand you the keys.** (dash-free form, see §15)
- CAMPAIGN lines (section heads, ads, one per surface): "The giants have armies. Now you do." · "Big-company power. Small-company soul." · "Own the machine." · "Built to be handed over."
Rules: one tagline per viewport. SIGNATURE never shares a page with a CAMPAIGN line. DESCRIPTOR never tries to inspire.

**The three brand pillars (every section on the site maps to exactly one; if it cannot be assigned, cut it):**
1. **PRAESIDIUM, the Protector.** Tribune stands between small businesses and a market where big-company AI is the price of survival. The enemy in all messaging is THE IMBALANCE, never big companies themselves.
2. **STRUCTURA, the Builder.** Not advice, not seats. A complete working AI organization built around the constraint actually bleeding the business.
3. **DISCESSUS, the Departer.** The engagement is designed to end. Success is measured by not being needed.

**The boilerplate paragraph (footer / about / press, brand verbatim in dash-free form, §15):**
Tribune Inc. builds complete AI organizations for small and mid-size businesses, designed around each company's costliest constraint, installed on hardware the client owns, and trained until they pass a formal Independence Test. Then Tribune hands over the keys and leaves. Tribune Inc. is a Delaware corporation. TribuneOS.com.

**The two-layer rule (governs all storytelling):** every surface holds a modern layer (flat geometry, precise type, engineered language: the HOW) over a Roman spine (the Tribune of the Plebs: protector of ordinary citizens, door always open, returns power when the term ends: the WHY). The Roman layer lives in meaning, naming, the motto, and the ceremonial face only. Never literal Roman kitsch: no columns, no laurels, no togas, no Trajan. Never fasces, eagles, or imperial iconography. The tribunate was plebeian and anti-imperial. Shield yes, empire no.

**The three ideas the whole site is built on (Jeff's brief):**
1. **Light.** Everything the agent does is visible. Glass box, not black box. You see every decision before it executes. Also the LIGHT principle from the soul layer, translated publicly: we build to help people become the greatest version of themselves, including the people inside the businesses we serve.
2. **Soul.** Every agent ships with a written soul: why it exists, who it serves, and what it refuses to do. Not a config file. A purpose.
3. **Purpose.** An agent that knows why it exists makes better calls than one that only knows what to do. Our agents think well of the world and act like it. The opposite of the movie AI: the AI in the movies wants to take over. Ours is built to hand over.

## 2. Voice, Tone & Word List

**Voice:** calm, concrete, confident, warm. Short sentences. Plain verbs. The reader is a business owner, not a technologist. No hype words, no exclamation marks. Explain like the smartest tradesperson they know: someone who could show off but chooses to be clear.

**Punctuation rule (site-wide):** no em dashes and no en dashes anywhere in published copy. Use periods, commas, or colons.

| We say | We never say |
|---|---|
| AI agents, your agents, your system | bots, AGI, superintelligence, "the AI" |
| carries the work, takes it off your plate | replaces your staff, eliminates headcount |
| you own it, it runs without us | ongoing dependency framing of any kind |
| glass box, every decision visible | black box (except when naming what we are not) |
| built for your rules (HIPAA, GDPR) | "fully compliant" as a bare claim without the architecture explanation |
| a written purpose, a soul | consciousness, sentience, feelings claims |
| declines and asks a human | autonomous (unqualified) |

**Banned vocabulary (brand §8, absolute):** "agentic transformation", "leverage", "seamless", "cutting-edge", "revolutionize", "paradigm", "unlock", "supercharge", "AI-powered" as a modifier, "journey".

**Register: authority with an edge.** Calm, declarative, specific to the dollar. Dollars, not buzzwords. Bad news first, with the fix attached. Short sentences win. Teach, don't gatekeep: every explanation leaves the reader more capable. Zero exclamation marks, no emoji on brand surfaces. Sentence case for headings and buttons; the wordmark and nav labels are uppercase. Oxford comma always. Numbers as numerals when they are money or metrics.

**Persona tuning (every page names its target):**
| Persona | Core anxiety | Winning message |
|---|---|---|
| The Operator-Owner (primary) | Being sold "AI" instead of a fix | Dollars, the fix, and what happens when Tribune leaves |
| The Growth-Stage Founder | Renting capability forever | Ownership language: your hardware, your accounts, your agents |
| The Trusted Manager (gatekeeper) | Being replaced by the system | The handoff promotes them: they end the engagement running the machine |
At least one section or FAQ answer on the site speaks directly to the Trusted Manager's fear. See §4.7 and the FAQ.

### 3.2 Header 1 (small utility bar)
`About · Location · Reach out`
- About → /about
- Location → dropdown or single link listing served locations, each to its localized landing page. Also carries the language switch (EN/DE) when locales exist for the current page.

### 3.3 Header 2 (big primary nav)
`Builds · Functions · Details`
- **Builds** → /builds. The showcase: the agent archetypes as cards, each to a detail page. This is the heart of the site.
- **Functions** → /functions. The same builds sliced by what they do (recover money owed, handle incoming paperwork, watch and report, keep the books straight, guard deadlines, produce and deliver). Each function links to the builds that carry it. Serves visitors who think in tasks, not industries.
- **Details** → dropdown: How we build · Proof · Doctrine: Light, Soul & Purpose · Trust & compliance · FAQ.

**Nav treatment (brand):** Header 2 labels render uppercase in the mono face, 12px, letter-spacing +32% (`BUILDS · FUNCTIONS · DETAILS`). In Header 1, "Reach out" carries the accent (tyrian-deep text on the light ground) and behaves as the site's START. The brand's recommended nav (METHOD · DOCTRINE · PROOF · START) maps into this structure: Method, Doctrine, and Proof live under Details; Reach out is Start.

### 3.4 Footer
Boilerplate paragraph (§0) · nav repeat · locations · legal links (per locale) · no social proof logos of clients (guardrail) · a single quiet line: "Our greatest success is the day you no longer need us."

### 4.6a The Imbalance band (draft)
Section head: **The giants have armies. Now you do.** (CAMPAIGN-1)
Copy: The giants have armies of agents. The neighborhood practice, the regional distributor, the ten-person firm get chatbots and subscriptions. The tools to level that field exist. They are just sold as rent. We build you the machine, in your name, on your hardware, and hand you the keys. The enemy here is the imbalance, never the companies that got there first.

### 4.6b Proof points (in this order of strength, always)
1. **The Independence Test.** Five business days, real operations, zero Tribune intervention, mechanical pass or fail. The only exam in the industry a vendor designs to make itself unnecessary.
2. **Ownership, literally.** Hardware on your premises, accounts in your name, repositories in your control, from day one, not at handoff. Tribune's access ends; the system doesn't.
3. **The Senate.** Every architecture is attacked by multiple independent AI models before a client sees it. Agreement is computed from observed critiques. A design must survive scrutiny, not collect applause.
4. **The engagement ends.** A fixed arc with human-signed gates and a scheduled departure. The business model with nothing to renew is the business model you can trust.

### 4.6c Competitive frame (table, verbatim structure)
| They say | The catch | Tribune says |
|---|---|---|
| Consultants: "We'll advise you." | The retainer never ends. | We build it, prove it, and leave. |
| SaaS: "Subscribe to capability." | You rent forever; they own the system. | You own the hardware, data, and agents outright. |
| DIY tools: "Anyone can build agents." | Someone in-house becomes the unpaid AI department. | We build it right, then train your people to run it. |

### 4.7 The Trusted Manager block (draft)
Eyebrow: FOR THE PERSON WHO RUNS THE OFFICE
Copy: If you are the one who keeps this place running, here is what this means for you: you are not being replaced, you are being promoted. The training ladder ends with you operating the system. The recovery test is passed by you, unaided. The handoff report carries your signature next to the owner's. When we leave, you are the one holding the keys.

### 4.5 Purpose band (draft)

### 5.3 The shared never list (verbatim, all builds)
> **What this agent will never do.**
> It will not take an irreversible action without a named human's sign-off.
> It will not spend a cent without approval.
> It will not send anything to a payer, a customer, or a court without confirmation.
> It will not guess. When it is unsure, it says so and asks the right person.
> It will not police or blame your people. When something fails, we fix the system, not the person.
> It will not move your data anywhere it does not belong.

### 5.4 Seeded builds (six detail pages)

**Build 1: The Recovery Agent** `/builds/the-recovery-agent` · default edition: Regulated
- H1: **Money you earned. Recovered before lunch.**
- Sub: Every day, revenue leaks out of your business through denials, rejections, and invoices nobody had time to chase. The Recovery Agent chases all of it, and brings your team a signed-off plan instead of a backlog.
- Pain: "Denied claims sit because everyone is busy." · "Deadlines pass and recoverable money becomes unrecoverable." · "You find out at month end, when it is too late."
- Day vignette (illustrative): 7:02 AM. Before your coffee, your agent has read every overnight remittance, sorted what is recoverable from what is not, drafted the corrections, attached each filing deadline, and queued the lot for your biller's sign-off. Nothing moves without a human. Nothing waits on one either.
- Capabilities: reads remittances and payment files · classifies every denial by reason code · separates recoverable from write-off · drafts corrections and appeals · attaches the deadline to every task · posts clean status to your morning brief · escalates anything unfamiliar to a human, with what it knows
- Soul excerpt: *"I exist to recover the revenue this practice earns but loses to friction, and to give the billing team their afternoons back."*
- Verticals: medical practice (Regulated, records never leave the building) · services and trades (Standard, unpaid-invoice variant)
- Image direction: a biller's desk at golden morning light, inbox at zero, coffee still hot.

**Build 2: The Intake Agent** `/builds/the-intake-agent` · edition by data type
- H1: **Everything that arrives, handled.**
- Sub: Faxes, referrals, orders, forms, email attachments. The Intake Agent reads them the moment they land, checks them for completeness, and routes them where they belong. Incomplete paperwork gets caught at the door, not discovered downstream where it costs real money.
- Pain: "Someone retypes what a machine already printed." · "A missing signature today is a denial in six weeks." · "Nobody knows what came in until someone opens it."
- Soul excerpt: *"I exist so nothing that arrives gets lost, and no one spends their day retyping what a machine can read."*
- Image direction: a front desk, morning, paper tray empty, the person there talking to a patient instead of a scanner.

**Build 3: The Signal Agent** `/builds/the-signal-agent` · default edition: Standard
- H1: **Walk in knowing more.**
- Sub: Your accounts leave a trail: hiring, funding, leadership moves, quiet signals that a deal is warming or cooling. The Signal Agent watches all of it and briefs your team every morning, and before every call.
- Pain: "Research eats selling time." · "The signal was public. You saw it a week late." · "Every rep preps differently, some not at all."
- Soul excerpt: *"I exist so this team walks into every conversation knowing more than the other side expects."*
- Image direction: a seller at dawn reviewing one crisp brief, not forty tabs.

**Build 4: The Ledger Agent** `/builds/the-ledger-agent` · edition by data type
- H1: **Books that close themselves. Almost.**
- Sub: From your operational systems to your ledger, cleanly, daily. Summary entries posted to the right accounts, reconciliation flagged the day it drifts, and a month end that takes an afternoon instead of a week.
- Soul excerpt: *"I exist so the books close clean and nobody spends Friday night reconciling."*

**Build 5: The Sentinel** `/builds/the-sentinel` · edition by data type
- H1: **The deadline that never surprises you again.**
- Sub: Licenses, credentials, renewals, filings, timely-filing windows. The Sentinel tracks every clock in your business and raises its hand early, loudly, and to the right person.
- Soul excerpt: *"I exist so the deadline that could hurt this business is never a surprise."*

**Build 6: The Producer** `/builds/the-producer` · default edition: Standard
- H1: **Client work out the door. On time, every time.**
- Sub: Recurring reports and deliverables assembled from your sources, checked against your standards, and staged for a human's final eye before anything ships. Your delivery promise, kept on schedule.
- Soul excerpt: *"I exist so the work your clients pay for goes out on time, checked by a human who had time to check it."*
