What it runs on

Three principles. One five-year argument.

01

Agents decide. Automations move. Databases remember.

Judgment goes to an agent. Anything that must be identical every time goes to a deterministic pipeline. State lives in a database the client owns. Confusing these three is the number-one reliability failure in AI systems.

02

Proven automation platforms, controlled and alarmed.

Deterministic work runs on proven automation infrastructure (Make.com and similar), every pipe with a failure alarm and a runbook. Tribune's own independent workflow engine, Lobster flows, inside the agent gateway, is currently in development and will progressively replace third-party platforms where reliability demands it.

03

The five-year argument: connect, don't replace.

Tribune connects the systems a business already uses: practice management, EHR, accounting, fax, portals, email. Nothing needs replacing; everything becomes connected. The architecture separates your operational model from any single generation of tools. A year ago the frontier was Make.com-class automation; today it is agent orchestration; in five years it will be something else. Tribune-built organizations adapt without a rebuild.

This is what we call Tribune OS: not a product page, not a subscription. An operational system that connects an organization's existing software, data, people, and AI into a continuously adapting operating model.
