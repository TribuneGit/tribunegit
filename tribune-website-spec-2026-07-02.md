## 0. Positioning Brief (read first, governs every page)

**What the public site says Tribune is:**
Tribune builds AI agents for businesses. We design them, train them on your real work, deploy them inside the tools your team already uses, and then hand you the keys. You own the system. It runs without us. That is the point.

**The moat vs. marketing rule (permanent, non-negotiable):**
- Internal truth (the moat): an AI builds other AI agents, and gets better with every build.
- Public message (the marketing): *we build AI agents for you.* A service, delivered by a firm.
- The website NEVER states, implies, or winks at the internal truth. No "the asset is the builder" framing, no AI C-suite roster, no meta-agent language. "We" on the website means the firm and its founders. Full stop.

**Primary domain:** TribuneInc.com (TribuneInc.com redirects).

**Tagline system (from the brand copy library, altitude rules apply):**
- SIGNATURE (the manifesto, verbatim): **We win when you no longer need us.** Used in the About close and the Doctrine close. Never on a page that carries a CAMPAIGN line.
- LOCKUP (footers, tight spaces): **Power that stays. Help that leaves.**
- DESCRIPTOR (subheads, meta descriptions): **We build your AI organization, then hand you the keys.** (dash-free form, see §15)
- CAMPAIGN lines (section heads, ads, one per surface): "The giants have armies. Now you do." · "Big-company power. Small-company soul." · "Own the machine." · "Built to be handed over."
Rules: one tagline per viewport. SIGNATURE never shares a page with a CAMPAIGN line. DESCRIPTOR never tries to inspire.

**The three brand pillars (every section on the site maps to exactly one; if it cannot be assigned, cut it):**
1. **PRAESIDIUM, the Protector.** Tribune stands between small businesses and a market where big-company AI is the price of survival. The enemy in all messaging is THE IMBALANCE, never big companies themselves.
2. **STRUCTURA, the Builder.** Not advice, not seats. A complete working AI organization built around the constraint actually bleeding the business.
3. **DISCESSUS, the Departer.** The engagement is designed to end. Success is measured by not being needed.

**The boilerplate paragraph (footer / about / press, brand verbatim in dash-free form, §15):**
Tribune Inc. builds complete AI organizations for small and mid-size businesses, designed around each company's costliest constraint, installed on hardware the client owns, and trained until they pass a formal Independence Test. Then Tribune hands over the keys and leaves. Tribune Inc. is a Delaware corporation. TribuneInc.com.

**The two-layer rule (governs all storytelling):** every surface holds a modern layer (flat geometry, precise type, engineered language: the HOW) over a Roman spine (the Tribune of the Plebs: protector of ordinary citizens, door always open, returns power when the term ends: the WHY). The Roman layer lives in meaning, naming, the motto, and the ceremonial face only. Never literal Roman kitsch: no columns, no laurels, no togas, no Trajan. Never fasces, eagles, or imperial iconography. The tribunate was plebeian and anti-imperial. Shield yes, empire no.

**The three ideas the whole site is built on (Jeff's brief):**
1. **Light.** Everything the agent does is visible. Glass box, not black box. You see every decision before it executes. Also the LIGHT principle from the soul layer, translated publicly: we build to help people become the greatest version of themselves, including the people inside the businesses we serve.
2. **Soul.** Every agent ships with a written soul: why it exists, who it serves, and what it refuses to do. Not a config file. A purpose.
3. **Purpose.** An agent that knows why it exists makes better calls than one that only knows what to do. Our agents think well of the world and act like it. The opposite of the movie AI: the AI in the movies wants to take over. Ours is built to hand over.

## 2. Voice, Tone & Word List

**Voice:** calm, concrete, confident, warm. Short sentences. Plain verbs. The reader is a business owner, not a technologist. No hype words, no exclamation marks. Explain like the smartest tradesperson they know: someone who could show off but chooses to be clear.

**Punctuation rule (site-wide):** no em dashes and no en dashes anywhere in published copy. Use periods, commas, or colons.

| We say | We never say |
|---|---|
| AI agents, your agents, your system | bots, AGI, superintelligence, "the AI" |
| carries the work, takes it off your plate | replaces your staff, eliminates headcount |
| you own it, it runs without us | ongoing dependency framing of any kind |
| glass box, every decision visible | black box (except when naming what we are not) |
| built for your rules (HIPAA, GDPR) | "fully compliant" as a bare claim without the architecture explanation |
| a written purpose, a soul | consciousness, sentience, feelings claims |
| declines and asks a human | autonomous (unqualified) |

**Banned vocabulary (brand §8, absolute):** "agentic transformation", "leverage", "seamless", "cutting-edge", "revolutionize", "paradigm", "unlock", "supercharge", "AI-powered" as a modifier, "journey".

**Register: authority with an edge.** Calm, declarative, specific to the dollar. Dollars, not buzzwords. Bad news first, with the fix attached. Short sentences win. Teach, don't gatekeep: every explanation leaves the reader more capable. Zero exclamation marks, no emoji on brand surfaces. Sentence case for headings and buttons; the wordmark and nav labels are uppercase. Oxford comma always. Numbers as numerals when they are money or metrics.

**Persona tuning (every page names its target):**
| Persona | Core anxiety | Winning message |
|---|---|---|
| The Operator-Owner (primary) | Being sold "AI" instead of a fix | Dollars, the fix, and what happens when Tribune leaves |
| The Growth-Stage Founder | Renting capability forever | Ownership language: your hardware, your accounts, your agents |
| The Trusted Manager (gatekeeper) | Being replaced by the system | The handoff promotes them: they end the engagement running the machine |
At least one section or FAQ answer on the site speaks directly to the Trusted Manager's fear. See §4.7 and the FAQ.

**One override:** the brand doc welcomes em dashes; this site does not. The no-dash rule (Jeff) wins on every published surface, including brand-verbatim lines, which are adopted in dash-free form (§15).

## 7. Doctrine Page. "Light, Soul & Purpose" (full draft copy)

`/details/purpose` — the doctrine page [DISCESSUS]. This is the page that answers the fear without ever naming a movie, and carries the Roman spine. Page order: the Roman story → the 2-minute narrative → Light / Soul / Purpose → the never list → the ceremonial close. Draft copy, ready to place:

> ### Why "Tribune"
> In Rome, a tribune held power for one purpose: to protect and strengthen the people he served, not to rule them. The office existed so ordinary citizens had a champion inside the system. The tribune's door stayed open, his person was protected so he could stand between power and the people, and when the term ended, the power went back. That is the name, and that is the job description. We build power for others. We keep none of it.

> ### The premise (the 2-minute narrative, dash-free form, §15)
> Every small business now competes with companies whose back offices run at machine speed. The tools exist to level that field, but they are sold as subscriptions that rent you capability, or consulting that never ends. We built Tribune on the opposite premise: that a business should own its operating power the way it owns its building.
>
> An engagement runs five phases. Discovery finds the constraint that actually costs you money: not the interesting problem, the expensive one. Architecture designs an AI organization around it, and every design survives an adversarial review by multiple independent AI models before you ever see it. Build installs it on your hardware, in your name. Train runs your system and your people up a ladder. We demonstrate, we shadow, we supervise, and then we go silent. Handoff is a formal Independence Test: five business days, real work, zero help from us. Pass, and we unplug: access revoked, keys handed over, one 30-day check-in. Fail, and we fix it and test again, because the test caught it while we are still in the room.
>
> We measure success in one way: you, running your own AI organization, without us. Rome had a word for the office that defends ordinary people and then returns its power. So do we.

> ### Light

## 8. Method Page. "How We Build" (draft)

`/details/method` — the five phases in the client's language. One horizontal timeline plus five short blocks:

1. **Discovery.** We sit inside your business for one to two weeks and follow the money: where a dollar enters, where it leaks, which tools you live in, what the rules are. It ends with a report so clear a stranger could understand your business from it. You confirm it before anything else happens. A discovery deposit starts the engagement and is credited toward your build.
2. **Architecture.** We design the smallest system that removes the biggest friction, and we put the math in front of you before we build. You approve the design in writing. For regulated businesses, the compliance architecture and the legal paperwork are settled here, before any real record is touched.
3. **Build.** On your hardware, in your accounts, with your keys. From day one you own every piece. We test everything, including on purpose breaking it, before your team ever relies on it.
4. **Train.** Your agents earn autonomy one workflow at a time: first we demonstrate, then they shadow, then they work supervised, then alone. Promotion is earned by clean repetitions, never by the calendar.
5. **Handoff.** Five business days of live operation. We watch in silence and do not touch anything. The system runs your real work, handles surprises, declines what it should decline, and your designated operator recovers from a staged failure using nothing but the manual. Pass is mechanical: zero critical issues, or it is not a pass. There is no conditional pass, and we do not soften the test to hit a date. Then we hand you the keys and unplug. If it fails, we fix it, wait, and run the whole test again.

**The Independence Promise (boxed):** You will never be dependent on us. If you later want us for updates and upgrades, that is a service you can choose. It is never a need we engineer.

## 9. Trust & Compliance Page (draft)

`/details/trust` — one page, three moves:

**Compliance by architecture, not by filter.** For regulated builds, the part of the system that touches protected records runs on a sealed machine inside your building: no internet path, no cloud access, physically incapable of leaking. The part that talks to the outside world never receives a record, by construction. Between the two sits one narrow, audited crossing that carries only approved, anonymized shapes. A filter can fail open. Architecture cannot.

**Your accounts, your keys.** Every account, key, and repository is created in your name from day one. We hold temporary access and revoke it at handoff. Credentials are placed by hand, never synced, never stored where an agent could read them.

**The audit trail.** Every action is logged, every decision reviewable, every crossing recorded. For healthcare clients we operate under a business associate agreement. For our German clients, the same architecture is what DSGVO wishes every vendor did: your data does not leave the house because it cannot.
