We know your practice

We know what your billing team is dealing with.

  • Prior authorization for nearly everything, and concurrent reviews every few days that can cut off a residential or PHP stay mid-treatment
  • Authorization expirations that slip through the cracks: one missed renewal date and a full week of per-diem revenue is gone
  • Behavioral carve-outs: claims bounced automatically because the plan routes mental health to a different payer than the card in your patient's hand
  • Medical-necessity reviews on ongoing therapy: payer algorithms flag your 90837 frequency and scanning notes for repeated language
  • Time-bound CPT codes and modifier rules where one wrong digit produces a clean, automatic denial
  • Appeals that could be won under federal parity law, but nobody on staff has the hours to research and draft them

Your practice is not broken. You're fighting an AI system with human hours. Tribune changes that.

Your EHR has AI. This is not that.

The Parity Argument Your Payer Hopes You Never Make.

Federal law, the Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act, requires payers to apply the same utilization management standards to behavioral health that they apply to medical and surgical care. Many routinely don't. And most practices never challenge it, because building a parity argument takes research and drafting hours no billing team has. Tribune's agents build that argument automatically, on every applicable denial. The law has been on your side the whole time. Now something in your office finally has the hours to use it.
