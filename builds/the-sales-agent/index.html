A Tribune build

No deal dies of neglect.

Deals rarely die in the meeting. They die in the week after it: the follow-up that slipped, the record nobody updated, the quiet account no one noticed going quiet. The Sales Agent runs the machine between the conversations, so your sellers can be in them.

The problem
  • Follow-ups depend on memory, and memory has a day job.
  • The CRM is always three weeks behind reality.
  • Deals stall silently. You find out at pipeline review.
A morning with your agent · illustrative

7:15 AM. Every deal that moved, stalled, or aged overnight is sorted. Three follow-ups are drafted and queued for your sellers' sign-off. Two stale accounts are flagged with a suggested next step. Yesterday's calls are logged in the CRM from your sellers' two-minute voice notes — structured, complete, searchable. Nobody typed anything.

What it does
  • Drafts and schedules follow-up sequences after every first contact, for human sign-off
  • Logs calls and meetings from your sellers' voice notes — transcribed, structured, filed
  • Keeps contacts, deal stages, and activity current without anyone touching the CRM
  • Flags stale deals, aging follow-ups, and dropped threads before they become lost revenue
  • Posts a morning pipeline brief: what moved, what stalled, what needs a human today
  • Escalates anything unfamiliar to a human, with what it knows

Built for any team that sells: professional services, agencies, distributors, consultancies, SaaS. Standard edition. Pairs naturally with The Signal Agent — one keeps you informed, the other keeps you moving.

What this agent will never do

  • It will not take an irreversible action without a named human's sign-off.
  • It will not spend a cent without approval.
  • It will not send anything outside your walls — to a customer, a payer, a court, or anyone else — without confirmation.
  • It will not guess. When it is unsure, it says so and asks the right person.
  • It will not police or blame your people. When something fails, we fix the system, not the person.
  • It will not move your data anywhere it does not belong.
Written purpose

I exist so no deal dies of neglect: every follow-up fires on time, every record stays current, and your sellers spend their hours selling, not typing.

How it fits your team

Questions owners ask

How is this different from The Signal Agent?

The Signal Agent watches the world outside and briefs your team — who is hiring, who got funded, what changed. The Sales Agent runs the machine inside — follow-ups, records, pipeline motion. Many sales teams run both: one so you walk in knowing more, one so nothing slips when you walk out.

Who owns this system?

You do. Hardware, accounts, keys, documentation. Our access ends at handoff, and the handoff is gated by a formal Independence Test your team passes without us.

What happens to my team?

They get more time selling. The agent carries the repeatable admin — logging, filing, scheduling follow-ups — so your sellers do the work only people can do: listening, reading the room, building trust.
