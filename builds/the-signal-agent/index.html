The problem
  • Research eats selling time.
  • The signal was public. You saw it a week late.
  • Every rep preps differently, some not at all.
A morning with your agent · illustrative

6:55 AM. One brief per person, not forty alerts. Which accounts moved overnight, what changed, and the one thing worth saying in the 10 o'clock call. Before each meeting, a one-page brief: who is in the room, what they announced, where the conversation left off.

What it does
  • Watches your named accounts and sources around the clock
  • Catches hiring, funding, leadership, and product signals
  • Writes one morning brief per person, ranked by what matters
  • Prepares a one-page brief before every scheduled call
  • Tracks which signals turned into conversations
  • Escalates anything ambiguous to a human, with context

Built for firms whose product is knowing things first. Standard edition by default.

What this agent will never do

  • It will not take an irreversible action without a named human's sign-off.
  • It will not spend a cent without approval.
  • It will not send anything outside your walls — to a customer, a payer, a court, or anyone else — without confirmation.
  • It will not guess. When it is unsure, it says so and asks the right person.
  • It will not police or blame your people. When something fails, we fix the system, not the person.
  • It will not move your data anywhere it does not belong.
Written purpose

I exist so this team walks into every conversation knowing more than the other side expects.

How it fits your team

A build is a small team, not a single tool: one lead agent your staff talks to, and two to four specialists behind it, each with a narrow job and a written purpose. Runs in your accounts, on infrastructure you own, with every action logged and reviewable. How we handle trust and compliance →

Questions owners ask

Who owns this system?

You do. Hardware, accounts, keys, documentation. Our access ends at handoff, and the handoff is gated by a formal Independence Test your team passes without us.

What happens to my team?

They get promoted by it. The training ladder ends with your people operating the system, and the recovery test is passed by your designated operator, unaided. We build agents that carry the repeatable work so your people can do the work only people can do.

Where does the information come from?

Public and licensed sources you approve, plus your own systems. Every line in a brief carries its source. Nothing is scraped from anywhere you have not signed off on.
