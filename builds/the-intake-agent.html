Everything that arrives, handled.

Faxes, referrals, orders, forms, email attachments. The Intake Agent reads them the moment they land, checks them for completeness, and routes them where they belong. Incomplete paperwork gets caught at the door, not discovered downstream where it costs real money.

The problem

What it costs you right now

Someone retypes what a machine already printed.
A missing signature today is a denial in six weeks.
Nobody knows what came in until someone opens it.
Illustrative

8:15 AM. Twelve items arrived overnight: four referrals, three prior auth requests, five fax attachments. By the time your front desk sits down, eleven are routed to the right folder with completeness confirmed. The twelfth has a note: missing patient date of birth, flagged back to the sender with a specific request. Your team opens clean work, not a sorting problem.

What it does

Six things, before anyone touches a file

  • 01 Reads incoming documents the moment they arrive
  • 02 Checks every submission for completeness against your rules
  • 03 Routes complete items to the right person or system
  • 04 Flags incomplete items at the door, with what is missing
  • 05 Logs every arrival with a timestamp and disposition
  • 06 Escalates anything outside its rules to a human, with context

What this agent will never do

  • It will not take an irreversible action without a named human's sign-off.
  • It will not spend a cent without approval.
  • It will not send anything to a payer, a customer, or a court without confirmation.
  • It will not guess. When it is unsure, it says so and asks the right person.
  • It will not police or blame your people. When something fails, we fix the system, not the person.
  • It will not move your data anywhere it does not belong.
Written purpose
"I exist so nothing that arrives gets lost, and no one spends their day retyping what a machine can read."
How it fits your team

One lead agent, two to four specialists behind it

The Intake Agent's lead is the single point of contact your staff talks to. Behind it, specialists handle different document types: faxes, secure messages, referral portals. Each specialist is trained to your completeness checklist and your routing rules.

Common questions

Things people ask before they start

Who owns the system?
You do. Accounts, keys, hardware, documentation. Our access ends at handoff.
Does the Intake Agent replace front desk staff?
No. It removes the sorting, retyping, and routing decisions from their day so they can spend that time with patients and callers. We measure success in returned hours and in the problems that never reach the desk.
What happens when something breaks after you leave?
Your operator has a manual covering restart, logs, and recovery, and passed a live recovery test before we left. If you want us on call anyway, there is an optional service plan. Optional is the operative word.

Ready to clear the inbox?

Tell us about what arrives and where it gets stuck. We will scope a discovery visit and show you what an intake build looks like for your operation.
