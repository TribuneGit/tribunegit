A Tribune build

Everything that arrives, handled.

Faxes, referrals, orders, forms, email attachments. The Intake Agent reads them the moment they land, checks them for completeness, and routes them where they belong. Incomplete paperwork gets caught at the door, not discovered downstream where it costs real money.

The problem
  • Someone retypes what a machine already printed.
  • A missing signature today is a denial in six weeks.
  • Nobody knows what came in until someone opens it.
A morning with your agent · illustrative

8:14 AM. A referral lands by fax. Before it would have reached the top of anyone's pile, it is read, checked for completeness, matched to the right record, and routed. The one missing a signature is already on its way back to the sender, politely, with the exact line that needs ink.

What it does
  • Reads faxes, referrals, orders, and attachments as they arrive
  • Classifies each document by type
  • Checks completeness against the receiving requirement
  • Matches each item to the right record in your system
  • Routes clean items and flags gaps with what is missing
  • Posts an intake summary to your morning brief

In the Regulated edition, documents that carry protected information are processed inside your building, on your hardware, with no path to the outside.

What this agent will never do

  • It will not take an irreversible action without a named human's sign-off.
  • It will not spend a cent without approval.
  • It will not send anything outside your walls — to a customer, a payer, a court, or anyone else — without confirmation.
  • It will not guess. When it is unsure, it says so and asks the right person.
  • It will not police or blame your people. When something fails, we fix the system, not the person.
  • It will not move your data anywhere it does not belong.
Written purpose

I exist so nothing that arrives gets lost, and no one spends their day retyping what a machine can read.

How it fits your team

A build is a small team, not a single tool: one lead agent your staff talks to, and two to four specialists behind it, each with a narrow job and a written purpose. Your records never leave your building. Reasoning on protected data happens on a sealed machine, on site, with no path to the internet. How we handle trust and compliance →

Questions owners ask

Who owns this system?

You do. Hardware, accounts, keys, documentation. Our access ends at handoff, and the handoff is gated by a formal Independence Test your team passes without us.

What happens to my team?

They get promoted by it. The training ladder ends with your people operating the system, and the recovery test is passed by your designated operator, unaided. We build agents that carry the repeatable work so your people can do the work only people can do.

What if a document is unreadable?

It goes to a person, immediately, flagged as unreadable, with the source and timestamp attached. Nothing is silently dropped. Silent failure is the one thing we engineer against hardest.
