Regulated edition

Money you earned. Recovered before lunch.

Every day, revenue leaks out of your business through denials, rejections, and invoices nobody had time to chase. The Recovery Agent chases all of it, and brings your team a signed-off plan instead of a backlog.

The problem

What it costs you right now

Denied claims sit because everyone is busy.
Deadlines pass and recoverable money becomes unrecoverable.
You find out at month end, when it is too late.
Illustrative

7:02 AM. Before your coffee, your agent has read every overnight remittance, sorted what is recoverable from what is not, drafted the corrections, attached each filing deadline, and queued the lot for your biller's sign-off. Nothing moves without a human. Nothing waits on one either.

What it does

Seven things, done before your team arrives

  • 01 Reads remittances and payment files
  • 02 Classifies every denial by reason code
  • 03 Separates recoverable from write-off
  • 04 Drafts corrections and appeals
  • 05 Attaches the deadline to every task
  • 06 Posts clean status to your morning brief
  • 07 Escalates anything unfamiliar to a human, with what it knows

What this agent will never do

  • It will not take an irreversible action without a named human's sign-off.
  • It will not spend a cent without approval.
  • It will not send anything to a payer, a customer, or a court without confirmation.
  • It will not guess. When it is unsure, it says so and asks the right person.
  • It will not police or blame your people. When something fails, we fix the system, not the person.
  • It will not move your data anywhere it does not belong.
Written purpose
"I exist to recover the revenue this practice earns but loses to friction, and to give the billing team their afternoons back."
How it fits your team

A small team behind a single point of contact

A build is a small team: one lead agent your staff talks to, plus two to four specialists behind it. The Recovery Agent's lead handles your morning brief and your biller's queue. The specialists read remittances, classify codes, and draft appeals.

Common questions

Things people ask before they start

Who owns the system?
You do. Accounts, keys, hardware, documentation. Our access ends at handoff.
Do your agents replace billing staff?
No. They carry the repeatable work so your billers can do the work only people can do. We measure success in returned hours, and in systems staff are glad to work alongside.
What happens when something breaks after you leave?
Your operator has a manual covering restart, logs, and recovery, and passed a live recovery test before we left. If you want us on call anyway, there is an optional service plan. Optional is the operative word.

Ready to start recovering?

Tell us about your billing operation. We will scope a discovery visit and show you exactly what this build would recover in your practice.
