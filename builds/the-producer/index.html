The problem
  • The monthly report eats the last three days of every month.
  • Every deliverable is assembled by hand from the same five sources.
  • Quality depends on who had time.
A morning with your agent · illustrative

The 28th, 2:00 PM. The month's deliverables are assembled, checked against your standard, and waiting in the review queue with two flagged items and the reason they were flagged. A person approves. Then, and only then, it ships.

What it does
  • Pulls from your named sources on schedule
  • Assembles each deliverable to your template and standard
  • Checks it against your rules before a human sees it
  • Stages it for one human review, then delivers
  • Tracks the delivery promise and reports on it
  • Flags source problems the day they start

What this agent will never do

  • It will not take an irreversible action without a named human's sign-off.
  • It will not spend a cent without approval.
  • It will not send anything to a payer, a customer, or a court without confirmation.
  • It will not guess. When it is unsure, it says so and asks the right person.
  • It will not police or blame your people. When something fails, we fix the system, not the person.
  • It will not move your data anywhere it does not belong.
Written purpose

I exist so the work your clients pay for goes out on time, checked by a human who had time to check it.

How it fits your team

A build is a small team, not a single tool: one lead agent your staff talks to, and two to four specialists behind it, each with a narrow job and a written purpose. Runs in your accounts, on infrastructure you own, with every action logged and reviewable. How we handle trust and compliance →

Questions owners ask

Who owns this system?

You do. Hardware, accounts, keys, documentation. Our access ends at handoff, and the handoff is gated by a formal Independence Test your team passes without us.

What happens to my team?

They get promoted by it. The training ladder ends with your people operating the system, and the recovery test is passed by your designated operator, unaided. We build agents that carry the repeatable work so your people can do the work only people can do.

Does anything ship without review?

No. Every deliverable waits for a human approval before it leaves the building. That gate does not relax over time. It is the point.
