The imbalance

The giants have armies. Now you do.

The giants have armies of agents. The neighborhood practice, the regional distributor, the ten-person firm get chatbots and subscriptions. The tools to level that field exist. They are just sold as rent. We build you the machine, in your name, on your hardware, and hand you the keys. The enemy here is the imbalance, never the companies that got there first.

Proof, in order of strength
01

The Independence Test

Five business days, real operations, zero Tribune intervention, mechanical pass or fail. The only exam in the industry a vendor designs to make itself unnecessary.

02

Ownership, literally

Hardware on your premises, accounts in your name, repositories in your control, from day one, not at handoff. Tribune's access ends. The system doesn't.

03

The Senate

Every architecture is attacked by multiple independent AI models before a client sees it. Agreement is computed from observed critiques. A design must survive scrutiny, not collect applause.

04

The engagement ends

A fixed arc with human-signed gates and a scheduled departure. The business model with nothing to renew is the business model you can trust.
