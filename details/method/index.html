How we build

Five phases. Human-signed gates. A scheduled departure.

Discovery→Architecture →Build→ Train→Handoff

1. Discovery

We sit inside your business for one to two weeks and follow the money: where a dollar enters, where it leaks, which tools you live in, what the rules are. It ends with a report so clear a stranger could understand your business from it. You confirm it before anything else happens. A discovery deposit starts the engagement and is credited toward your build.

2. Architecture

We design the smallest system that removes the biggest friction, and we put the math in front of you before we build. Every design is attacked by multiple independent AI models, the Senate, before you ever see it. You approve the design in writing. For regulated businesses, the compliance architecture and the legal paperwork are settled here, before any real record is touched.

3. Build

On your hardware, in your accounts, with your keys. From day one you own every piece. We test everything, including breaking it on purpose, before your team ever relies on it.

4. Train

Your agents earn autonomy one workflow at a time: first we demonstrate, then they shadow, then they work supervised, then alone. Promotion is earned by clean repetitions, never by the calendar. Your people train up the same ladder, and end it running the machine.

5. Handoff: the Independence Test

Five business days of live operation. We watch in silence and touch nothing. The system runs your real work, handles surprises, declines what it should decline, and your designated operator recovers from a staged failure using nothing but the manual. Pass is mechanical: zero critical issues, or it is not a pass. There is no conditional pass, and we do not soften the test to hit a date. Then we hand you the keys and unplug. If it fails, we fix it, wait, and run the whole test again.

The Independence Promise

You will never be dependent on us. If you later want us for updates and upgrades, that is a service you can choose. It is never a need we engineer.
