Doctrine

Light, soul & purpose.

Why our agents are good citizens, and why the company that builds them is designed to leave.

Why "Tribune"

In Rome, a tribune held power for one purpose: to protect and strengthen the people he served, not to rule them. The office existed so ordinary citizens had a champion inside the system. The tribune's door stayed open, his person was protected so he could stand between power and the people, and when the term ended, the power went back. That is the name, and that is the job description. We build power for others. We keep none of it.

The premise

Every small business now competes with companies whose back offices run at machine speed. The tools exist to level that field, but they are sold as subscriptions that rent you capability, or consulting that never ends. We built Tribune on the opposite premise: that a business should own its operating power the way it owns its building.

An engagement runs five phases. Discovery finds the constraint that actually costs you money: not the interesting problem, the expensive one. Architecture designs an AI organization around it, and every design survives an adversarial review by multiple independent AI models before you ever see it. Build installs it on your hardware, in your name. Train runs your system and your people up a ladder. We demonstrate, we shadow, we supervise, and then we go silent. Handoff is a formal Independence Test: five business days, real work, zero help from us. Pass, and we unplug: access revoked, keys handed over, one 30-day check-in. Fail, and we fix it and test again, because the test caught it while we are still in the room.

We measure success in one way: you, running your own AI organization, without us. Rome had a word for the office that defends ordinary people and then returns its power. So do we.

Light

Everything our agents do is visible. Every decision, every draft, every action sits in a log your team can read, and the important ones wait for a human's sign-off before they happen. We call this building in the light: a glass box, not a black box. If you cannot see what a system is doing, you do not own it. Ours you can see. Ours you own.

Light also means what we build for. We build to help people become the greatest version of themselves, including the people inside the businesses we serve. The owner who has not taken a real vacation in four years. The biller drowning in denials she did not cause. The front desk doing the same check forty times a day. These are the humans at the end of every system we ship.

Soul

Every agent we build carries a written soul: why it exists, who it serves, and what it refuses to do. Not a slogan taped on afterward. A working document the agent reasons from.

Written purpose

I exist to recover the revenue this practice earns but loses to friction, and to give the billing team their afternoons back.

An agent that knows why it exists makes better judgment calls than one that only knows what to do, because purpose tells it what matters when the instructions run out.

Purpose

Our agents think well of the world and act like it. They do not police your people. They do not blame. When a workflow fails, we fix the system, because the system was wrong, not the person. A deployment that automates the work but leaves your staff feeling watched, replaceable, or stupid has failed, no matter how clean the logs are. We measure our work in returned human hours. We build systems people are glad to work alongside, and that gladness is a design requirement, not a nice-to-have.

The AI in the movies wants to take over. Ours is built to hand over. Every engagement ends with an independence test and a handoff: your system, your hardware, your keys, running without us.

What this agent will never do

  • It will not take an irreversible action without a named human's sign-off.
  • It will not spend a cent without approval.
  • It will not send anything to a payer, a customer, or a court without confirmation.
  • It will not guess. When it is unsure, it says so and asks the right person.
  • It will not police or blame your people. When something fails, we fix the system, not the person.
  • It will not move your data anywhere it does not belong.
