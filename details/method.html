How we build

Five phases. One goal: the day you no longer need us.

Every Tribune engagement follows the same arc. Discovery, then architecture, then build, then train, then handoff. The arc ends with five days of live operation where Tribune watches in silence and does not touch anything. Pass the test, and you own a system that runs without us.

Typically six to ten weeks. Ends with an independence test: proof your system runs without us.

Phase 1

Discovery

We sit inside your business for one to two weeks and follow the money, the paperwork, and the friction. We are looking for the one constraint that, if removed, changes the most. We leave with a constraint map and a proposal: this build, this architecture, this cost.

Phase 2

Architecture

We design the smallest system that removes the biggest friction. Every proposed architecture is submitted to an independent review where multiple AI models attack it before a human sees it. A design must survive scrutiny, not collect applause. You sign off before any code is written.

Phase 3

Build

On your hardware, in your accounts, with your keys, from day one. We build in your environment, not ours. There is no migration on handoff. The system you are handed is the system you watched being built.

Phase 4

Train

Your agents earn autonomy one workflow at a time. Every capability is demonstrated, verified, and logged before the next one is unlocked. Your team trains alongside the system. By handoff, your operator knows the system well enough to recover it from a dead stop.

Phase 5

Handoff

Five business days of live operation. Tribune watches in silence and does not touch anything. Your operator runs the system. At the end of five days, we run a pass/fail check: did the system complete every assigned workflow correctly, without Tribune intervention? Pass, and the keys are yours. Fail, and we stay until it passes.

You will never be dependent on us. If you later want us for updates and upgrades, that is a service you can choose. It is never a need we engineer.
