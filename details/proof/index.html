Proof

Trust is built from stated limits and kept promises.

Four things we can prove, in order of strength.

01

The Independence Test

Five business days of live operation on your real work. We are present and silent. Zero intervention: if we have to step in, that is the result, and the test is failed. The criteria are written into your architecture spec at the start, so the bar is known from day one. Pass is mechanical. There is no conditional pass, and the test is never softened to hit a date. It is the only exam in the industry a vendor designs to make itself unnecessary.

02

Ownership, literally

Hardware on your premises. Accounts, keys, and repositories in your name from day one, not transferred at the end. We hold temporary collaborator access and revoke it at handoff. Tribune's access ends. The system doesn't.

03

The Senate

Before you see a design, it survives an adversarial review by multiple independent AI models from different providers. They critique, stress-test, and attack it, and agreement is computed from what the critiques actually say, not asserted. A design must survive scrutiny, not collect applause.

04

The engagement ends

A fixed arc with human-signed gates and a scheduled departure, followed by one 30-day check-in. If you want managed help afterward, it is a service you choose, separately priced, never a dependency we engineer. The business model with nothing to renew is the business model you can trust.
