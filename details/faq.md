## Frequently Asked Questions

**Keywords:** Tribune AI FAQ, who owns AI agent, AI cost, what happens team AI, AI after handoff, Tribune questions

**Who owns the system?**
You do. Hardware, accounts, keys, documentation. Our access ends at handoff, and the handoff is gated by a formal Independence Test your team passes without us.

**What does it cost to get started?**
Engagements start with a discovery deposit. The total investment is scoped in discovery — the number you see before we build is the number in your contract. No surprises, no scope creep.

**What happens to my team?**
They get promoted by it. The training ladder ends with your people operating the system, and the Independence Test is passed by your designated operator, unaided. Agents carry the repeatable work so your people can do the work only people can do.

**What happens after Tribune leaves?**
Your team owns the system. Tribune's access ends at handoff. The Independence Test ensures it runs without us before we leave. You have full documentation, keys, and a trained operator.

**Is Tribune only for medical practices?**
No. The Regulated edition exists for businesses with protected records — medical, legal — because they are the hardest case, and we built for the hardest case first. The same builds run in their Standard edition for any business: sales teams, agencies, distributors, consultancies, service firms. Whatever is bleeding your business, start there.

**How long does a build take?**
Discovery takes one to two weeks. Build and training varies by complexity. The Independence Test is five business days. The full arc is typically eight to sixteen weeks depending on the build and your team's availability.
