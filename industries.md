## Same architecture. Different field.

Tribune applies the same core system across regulated industries: agents built around your costliest constraint, installed on hardware you own, trained to pass an Independence Test. What changes per field is the workflow, the compliance stack, and the knowledge base the agents run on.

## Current Industries

### Orthotics & Prosthetics

**URL:** https://tribuneinc.com/orthotics-and-prosthetics/  
**Edition:** Regulated

Payers use AI to deny O&P claims faster than staff can respond. Tribune builds the counter-system: denial recovery agents that work your rejections before lunch, intake agents that catch incomplete paperwork at the door, and compliance architecture that keeps PHI on your hardware.

**Focus areas:** HIPAA, prior authorization, denial recovery, intake, credentialing

---

### Behavioral Health

**URL:** https://tribuneinc.com/behavioral-health/  
**Edition:** Regulated

Behavioral health claims are denied at nearly double the rate of medical claims, and parity law violations go uncontested because fighting them takes time practices do not have. Tribune builds agents to do both: recover the revenue and flag the pattern.

**Focus areas:** HIPAA, 42 CFR Part 2, mental health parity, denial recovery, prior authorization

## How the Regulated Edition Works

For regulated builds, reasoning on records happens on a sealed machine in your building, with no path to the internet. PHI never leaves your walls. The compliance stack is settled before the build begins, not patched in after.
